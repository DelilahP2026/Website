Crochet Projects

There are many different types of crochet projects and they range from quick beginner friedly items to complex garments and blankets. People also commonly crochet unique designs and objects as room decor or just for fun!

Clothing Projects

Crochet projects can be quick, one day items like scrunchies, coasters, and chapsick holders to larger, long term endeavors like blankets and garments. Some popular beginner friendly options include dishcloths, granny squares, scarves, bandanas, soap saver bags, keychains, laptop cases, headbands, bracelets, and beginner amiguruimi projects.

Home Decor Projects

Home decor crochet projects are a fun and depending on what you make, sometimes easy. They can range from funcional storage to decorative accents. Popular items include storage baskets, pillows, blankets, plant hangers, garlands, coasters, rugs, stuffed animals, and other creative and unique projects that add personalized style to spaces.

Amigurumi & Toys Projects

Amigurumi originates from Japan, with the term combining the Japanese words ami (crocheting/knitted) and nuigurumi (stuffed doll). Key techniques include using a "magic ring" as a start, increasing and decreasing for shaping, and stitches.

What I Personally Started with

I personally started with bracelets and simple headbands, experimenting with different stitches I learned from the internet. For magic circles, I started doing amiguruimi stuffed plushes kits which helped me understand increasing, decreasing, simple stitches, and how things look based on what stitches and pattern of increasing and decreasing did. I also tried freehanding legwarmers, and ended up frogging (ripping out stitches to undo work) the entire thing because I ended up not liking the color and overall shape. I suggest doing amiguruimi to start instead of granny squares because in my personal opinion it was more entertaining and helped me know what each stitch and pattern would make the project look like in the end. Something I wish I knew before I started crocheting is that it's totally okay to be wrong in the beginning and to not totally know what you're doing because you will learn and improve. Overall, I'm so happy I learned how to crochet and hope your journey with that is fun!
